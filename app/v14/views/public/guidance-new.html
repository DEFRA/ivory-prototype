Your legal responsibilities under the Ivory Act 2018

The Ivory Act 2018 makes it illegal to deal in most items that contain elephant ivory. There are some exemptions.

! Warning You can be fined up to £250,000 and sent to prison if you sell, buy or otherwise deal in ivory that does not meet the exemption criteria.

If your item is exempt, you must tell us as soon as you want to:

  • sell or hire it out
  • offer to sell or hire it out
  • exchange it for goods or services
  • keep it for sale or hire
  • export or import it with the intention to sell or hire it out

Items that are exempt from the ivory ban

Exemption category How you need to tell us How long exemption lasts
1. Musical instrument made before 1975 with less than 20% ivory by volume Register the item online Single-use only (The new owner must re-register the item if they ever want to sell or hire it out)
2. Item made before 1947 and with less than 10% ivory by volume Register the item online Single-use only (The new owner must re-register the item if they ever want to sell or hire it out)
3. Portrait miniature made before 1918 with a surface area smaller than 320 square centimetres Register the item online Single-use only (The new owner must re-register the item if they ever want to sell or hire it out)
4. Item that a qualifying museum intends to buy or hire Register the item online Single-use only (The new owner must re-register the item if they ever want to sell or hire it out)
5. Item made before 1918 that is rare and culturally, historically or artistically important Apply online for a certificate from an approved expert Certificate stays with the item forever.

Use our online service to tell us about an item that you want to sell or hire out.

If you are buying or hiring an ivory item from someone else, you must be certain that the item is exempt before you proceed.

Items you need to register

You can complete the process for someone else.

You will be asked to:

  • provide photos and descriptions of the item
  • legally declare that the item meets the exemption criteria
  • pay an administration fee of £xx

We will:

  • immediately issue a registration number for the item
  • retrospectively check registrations to ensure they comply with the law

This registration expires as soon as you sell or hire it out. If the item is sold or hired out again, the owner will need to make a new registration.

Register an ivory item.

Items that need a certificate

You will be asked to:

  • provide photos and descriptions of the item
  • explain why the item is rare and important
  • provide documentation to support your claim
  • pay an administration fee of £xxx

We will:

  • send the application for checking by an approved expert
  • provide a certificate if the expert agrees with you

This certificate does not expire. When you sell the item, you must transfer the certificate to the new owner.

Apply for a certificate for a rare and important ivory item.

Other legal responsibilities

As part of the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES), you may also need to buy a permit or certificate to trade an item made from an endangered species.
