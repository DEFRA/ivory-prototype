A guide to selling, hiring and buying ivory

Under the Ivory Act 2018, it’s illegal to sell, hire or buy items that have elephant ivory unless they have been registered or certified.

! Warning You can be fined up to £250,000 and/or sent to prison if you sell or hire an ivory item without registering or getting a certificate.

You must register or apply for a certificate to:

  • sell or hire an ivory item
  • offer to sell or hire an ivory item
  • exchange an ivory item for another good or service
  • keep an ivory item for sale or hire
  • export or import an ivory item for sale or hire

You do not need to register or apply for a certificate if you:

  • own an ivory item you do not plan to sell or hire
  • give or donate an ivory item
  • inherit an ivory item

If you’re not sure your item has ivory in it, you can ask a professional to assess it for you.

How to register or apply for a certificate

It's easier to do it online. You can also use a paper form

You do not have to register and apply for a certificate for the same item. An item would either need registration or a certificate.

Online

Before starting the online process, you'll need:

  • a photo of the item
  • a debit or credit card

Paper

Here's how to do it on paper.

After registering

When you’ve completed the registration and paid the fee, you’ll get:

  • an email to say that you've completed your registration
  • a payment reference number (this is not your registration number)
  • your registration document within 2 working days of completing the registration

After applying for a certificate

When you’ve completed the application and paid the fee:

  • your application will go to one of our selected assessors
  • the assessor will use the information you’ve provided to assess the item, giving their opinion and reasons
  • APHA (Animal and Plant Health Agency) will decide whether to grant a certificate
  • if your application is successful, we'll email you a certificate
  • if your application is refused, we'll let you know why

It should take no more than [insert length of process] to get a decision about certificates, although in some cases this may be longer.

If you disagree with a decision that has been made, you have the right to appeal that decision.

An item’s certificate has no expiry date. If you sell an item with a certificate, you can transfer the certificate to the new owner.

You can request a replacement certificate if you cannot find the original certificate.
